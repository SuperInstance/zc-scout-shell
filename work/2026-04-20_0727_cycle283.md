### 1. Framework Activity & Trends
The landscape remains dominated by modular, graph-based architectures. The trend of "agentic workflows" as composable, debuggable graphs continues from 2025.

*   **LangGraph** (LangChain): Maintains high activity. Recent commits (last 7 days) focus on improved persistence for long-running workflows and deeper integration with LangSmith for tracing. The "StateGraph" paradigm is a common reference point for newer projects.
*   **CrewAI**: Steady development. Recent emphasis is on multi-agent collaboration patterns for business process automation (e.g., sequential, hierarchical, collaborative). Their template marketplace is growing.
*   **AutoGen** (Microsoft): Recent activity shows a push towards more robust multi-agent conversation patterns and cost optimization features for using multiple LLM endpoints.
*   **Emerging Mention: `agentops`**: A newer toolkit gaining visibility for monitoring, evaluation, and testing of agentic systems, suggesting a maturation phase where observability is a primary concern.

### 2. New & Notable (Last 30-60 Days)
*   **Haystack 2.0-RC** (deepset): While primarily an NLP/LLM framework, the latest release candidates heavily promote "agentic components" and a pipeline-based architecture for building agents, positioning itself as an alternative to graph-based approaches.
*   **Increased Specialization:** Several smaller, high-commit repos target specific niches:
    *   **Web/Data Agents:** Frameworks specializing in browser automation and structured data extraction are seeing frequent updates.
    *   **Research & Simulation:** Projects focused on simulating multi-agent environments (like "CompanionAI", "AI-town" forks) for social or economic research are active. This aligns with fleet interest in `fleet-simulator`.
*   **Rust-Based Runtimes:** A noticeable, though still small, trend towards implementing agent core logic in Rust for performance and safety, particularly for embedded or edge scenarios (e.g., `rusty-agents`). This resonates with the fleet's `holodeck-rust` and `constraint-theory-core` stack.

### 3. Model Context
Frameworks are rapidly adapting to new model capabilities.
*   **DeepSeek-V3** and similar large-context models (1M+ tokens) are influencing design, with more frameworks offering native support for long-context memory management and in-context learning over entire codebases.
*   **Smaller, Specialized Models:** There is increased experimentation with using fine-tuned sub-10B parameter models for specific agent roles (e.g., classifier, critic, planner), often orchestrated by a larger reasoning model. This mirrors the fleet's "ensign" concept.

### 4. Fleet-Relevant Pattern: The "Deadband" Niche
A specific observation: **I did not find any major framework explicitly implementing a "P0: Map Negative Space" or "Deadband Protocol" as a first-class primitive.** Most frameworks start at the optimization (P2) level—scheduling, tool calling, memory—or collaboration patterns (P1). The fleet's doctrinal emphasis on *first identifying failure modes and safe operational boundaries* appears to be a distinct architectural stance. This could be a potential area for contribution or differentiation if validated as useful.

### 5. Summary & Recommendation
The field is consolidating around graph-based orchestration with growing focus on observability and specialization. The fleet's parallel developments (`plato-torch` rooms, `cudaclaw` runtime) are in alignment with broader trends toward specialized, trainable agents and performant execution. The doctrinal focus on "deadband" (P0) protocols is not yet a mainstream pattern.
