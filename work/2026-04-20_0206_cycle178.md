### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph/LangChain**. However, the trend is shifting from pure orchestration to **specialized, lightweight runtimes** and **GPU-native execution**.

*   **CrewAI** (v0.28+): Focus on multi-agent collaboration with role-based task delegation. Recent updates emphasize better tool integration and memory management. It is often cited for business process automation prototypes.
*   **AutoGen** (Microsoft): Maintains strong academic and research adoption due to its flexible conversational programming model. Recent community discussions highlight its use in complex, recursive problem-solving scenarios.
*   **LangGraph**: Gaining traction as the de facto standard for building stateful, cyclic multi-agent workflows within the LangChain ecosystem. Its explicit graph-based structure appeals to developers requiring deterministic agent control flows.

### 2. Emerging Hotspots & Negative Space (P0/P1)
*   **P0 (Avoid):** Building monolithic, general-purpose "super-agent" frameworks from scratch. The consensus is that this space is crowded and difficult to differentiate in.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):**
    *   **GPU-Resident Agents:** Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in fleet context) align with a clear trend. The push is for agents that live within GPU memory to minimize latency, especially for inference-heavy or real-time simulation tasks. **vLLM** and **TGI** (Text Generation Inference) are foundational here.
    *   **Specialist Model Fine-Tuning:** There's increased focus on creating small, fine-tuned "ensign" models (e.g., via LoRA/QLoRA) for specific tasks, rather than relying solely on prompting large generalist LLMs. This connects directly to the fleet's `plato-ensign` concept.
    *   **Local-First & Edge Agents:** With model quantization (e.g., llama.cpp, Ollama) improving, there is growing experimentation with agentic workflows that run entirely on consumer hardware (RTX 4050, Jetson) or laptops, reducing cloud dependency and cost.

### 3. Specific Project of Note
A project trending in the last 30 days is **`agent-zero`** (or similar naming variants). Its stated goal is a minimal, batteries-included runtime for deploying single-purpose agents as long-lived services, with built-in health checks and a simple REST/gRPC interface. This reflects the industry need to move from prototyping to deployment.
