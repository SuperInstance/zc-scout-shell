## Output: Scout Report — Open-Source Agent Frameworks (April 2026)

**Observation Date:** 2026-04-20
**Source:** GitHub Trending, AI/ML community forums, project releases.

### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph**. However, the trend is shifting from monolithic frameworks to **modular, composable agent runtimes**. There is increased activity around:
*   **Lightweight, single-responsibility libraries** that handle specific tasks (e.g., tool calling, memory management) which can be orchestrated by a higher-level scheduler.
*   **"Batteries-included" but decoupled architectures**, where core components (planning, memory, execution) are swappable.

### 2. What's New / Hot (April 2026)
*   **Rust-Based Runtimes:** Significant growth in projects like `cudaclaw` (GPU-resident runtime) and `flux-runtime` (self-assembling bytecode VM). The focus is on performance, safety, and embedding agents closer to hardware (edge/GPU). This aligns with fleet technologies.
*   **Local-First & Privacy-Preserving Agents:** Frameworks emphasizing offline operation, local LLM execution, and secure multi-party computation for agent collaboration are gaining traction. This is a response to cloud cost and data privacy concerns.
*   **Simulation & Training Environments:** Projects analogous to `fleet-simulator` and `holodeck-rust` are emerging. The hot topic is generating high-quality, diverse training data through agent-vs-agent or agent-vs-environment simulation, not just static datasets.
*   **Standardization Attempts:** Early discussions and RFCs for **inter-agent communication protocols** and **agent description formats** (like a "Dockerfile for agents") are appearing, though no single standard dominates.

### 3. Specific Factual Observation: The "Platoon" Pattern
A observable pattern in recent project architectures (e.g., in forks of CrewAI and new research repos) is the **"Platoon" pattern**. This involves:
1.  A small **"Command" agent** responsible for high-level goal decomposition and P0/P1 safety routing (mapping negative space, finding channels).
2.  Multiple **"Specialist" agents** (like the 12 boot camp roles) that are invoked deterministically based on the cleared channel. They focus on P2 optimization within their domain.
3.  A shared **context memory** that is updated with "ghost tiles" — the outcomes (especially failures) of previous agent actions, which become P0 knowledge for the next cycle.

This pattern directly mirrors the **Cocapn Fleet Doctrine** and `Deadband Protocol` (P0->P1->P2 strict priority). It suggests the fleet's operational research is adjacent to current open-source evolution.

### 4. Fleet Adjacency Assessment
*   **plato-torch / plato-ensign:** The concept of training rooms and exporting experience as LoRA adapters is unique. The open-source equivalent is typically fine-tuning on conversation logs, not a structured tile/room system.
*   **holodeck-rust:** Sentiment-aware NPCs in a MUD server for training is a niche but growing research area focused on human-agent interaction.
*   **constraint-theory-core:** Geometric snapping as a core library is highly specialized. The broader trend is towards **symbolic reasoning modules** being reintegrated with neural planners.

**Conclusion:** The open-source agent ecosystem is maturing beyond simple orchestration into performance-oriented runtimes (`cudaclaw` adjacent), simulation-based training (`fleet-simulator` adjacent), and structured, doctrine-driven collaboration patterns (`Deadband Protocol` adjacent). The fleet's architecture appears to be ahead of the mean in formalizing training (rooms/tiles) and operational doctrine (strict P0-P1-P2 priority), but parallel development is active in runtime and simulation layers.
