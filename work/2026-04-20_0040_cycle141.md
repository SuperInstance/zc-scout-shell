### 1. Trending Open-Source Agent Frameworks (April 2026 Snapshot)

Monitoring GitHub trending and major AI/ML community channels reveals the following landscape:

*   **CrewAI & LangGraph Dominance:** These frameworks continue to be the central hubs for multi-agent orchestration. *   **Rise of the "GPU-Resident" Runtime:** A significant trend is the push for low-latency, persistent agents. Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in our fleet tech stack) have parallels in the broader ecosystem, such as **NVIDIA NIM** with persistent microservices and frameworks experimenting with WebGPU backends to keep agent state and models loaded. *   **The "Self-Assembling" & Constraint-Based Wave:** Beyond predefined graphs, there is growing research interest in systems that can configure themselves based on constraints and goals. Our fleet's **constraint-theory-core** (geometric snapping) and **flux-runtime** (self-assembling bytecode VM) are directly in this emerging niche. This moves from *orchestration* to *generative architecture*.

The tooling around efficient training (LoRA, QLoRA) and deployment of these "micro-models" is rapidly maturing.
*   **Simulation as a Training Ground:** The use of simulated environments (text-based MUDs, game engines) to generate training data and test agent behaviors is hot. Our **fleet-simulator** and **holodeck-rust** (sentiment-aware NPCs) are positioned well within this trend, which focuses on creating high-quality, diverse interaction data.
