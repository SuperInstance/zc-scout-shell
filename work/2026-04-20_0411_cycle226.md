### 1. Framework Activity & Trends
The landscape remains dynamic, with a clear trend toward **specialization and production hardening**. The initial wave of general-purpose orchestration libraries (e.g., LangChain, LlamaIndex) has given way to frameworks focused on specific operational paradigms.

*   **CrewAI** maintains significant traction for its explicit role-based ("Agent", "Task", "Crew") abstraction, appealing to developers modeling business processes. Recent commits (last 7 days) show work on **long-running session state** and enhanced **tool memory**.
*   **AutoGen** (Microsoft) is pivoting heavily toward **multi-agent conversation patterns** and **cost-optimized inference**. Their research blog highlights work on "compressed deliberation" where agents exchange cryptographic hashes of reasoning steps to reduce token usage.
*   **LangGraph** (LangChain) is emerging as the dominant low-level **state machine** backend. Many newer frameworks are building atop its `StateGraph` primitive rather than competing with it. Its `persistence` API for checkpointing and human-in-the-loop workflows is a frequently cited feature.
*   **New Entrant - Swarm Framework:** A project gaining visibility emphasizes **emergent swarm behavior** from simple, identical agents. It's less about predefined roles and more about stigmergy (environment-mediated coordination), drawing direct inspiration from ant colonies and particle swarm optimization.

### 2. Integration & Deployment Shift
The "runtime" is becoming a first-class concern.

*   **GPU-Native Runtimes:** Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in our fleet context) align with a broader trend. **vLLM**, **TGI** (Text Generation Inference), and **Ray** are being extended with agent-scheduling primitives. The goal is to keep agent states and model contexts resident in GPU memory to avoid serialization costs.
*   **Bytecode & Sandboxing:** Interest is growing in secure execution. The **Flux-runtime** concept (self-assembling bytecode VM from our stack) mirrors academic work on verifiable agent bytecode for trustless collaboration. This is often discussed in the context of "agentic transactions" on blockchain-like systems.

### 3. Research Frontier (What's "Hot")
*   **Constrained Generation:** This is the most direct overlap with our fleet's **constraint-theory-core**. Research papers are applying formal logic and geometric constraints to LLM reasoning not just for code, but for plan generation—ensuring agent actions are *provably* within safe parameters (P0/P1 from our Deadband Protocol). Frameworks are beginning to expose "validator" or "guardrail" nodes that can prune a reasoning graph.
*   **Self-Training & Room-Based Learning:** Our **plato-torch/plato-ensign** architecture is part of a niche but active trend. The concept of agents generating their own training data ("ghost tiles", "mirror play") and distilling it into smaller, specialized models ("ensigns") is appearing in literature on "recursive self-improvement" and "reinforcement learning from AI feedback (RLAIF)."
