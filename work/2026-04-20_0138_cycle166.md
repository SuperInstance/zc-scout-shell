### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Recent Shifts
The landscape continues to be defined by three primary architectural patterns, with a noticeable shift towards **lightweight, composable runtimes** over monolithic orchestrators.

*   **CrewAI & LangGraph** remain the most starred repositories for high-level, role-based multi-agent orchestration. However, recent commits (Q1 2026) show both focusing on **modular tooling** and **interoperability layers** rather than core API changes.
*   **AutoGen (Microsoft)** is seeing increased adoption in enterprise R&D contexts, particularly for its **conversable agent** patterns. The `autogen-ext` community package is trending, adding integrations with newer model providers and local LLM servers.
*   **Emerging Trend - GPU-Native Runtimes:** Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in our fleet knowledge) align with a broader movement. There is growing activity around frameworks that minimize CPU-GPU data transfer, aiming for sub-millisecond agent activation times. This is a P1 channel for latency-sensitive applications.

### 2. What's New & Hot (Q2 2026)
*   **Rust-Based Frameworks:** A significant spike in interest. **llm-chain** (Rust) and new entrants like **agent-rs** are gaining traction for their memory safety and performance in long-running, stateful agent processes. This correlates with our fleet's use of Rust in `holodeck-rust` and `constraint-theory-core`.
*   **"Model-Agnostic" Tooling:** The focus is shifting from the framework itself to the **tool layer**. Libraries that standardize tool definition, routing, and context management (e.g., **LangChain Tools**, **OpenAI's Function Calling** standard) are becoming critical dependencies. This creates a P0 constraint: new agent code must integrate with these de facto standards to leverage community tools.
*   **Local-First & Edge Agents:** Driven by more capable small models (e.g., DeepSeek Coder, Qwen 2.5 7B), there is increased framework support for **fully local execution**. This includes optimized inference backends (llama.cpp, vLLM) and frameworks that bundle a lightweight runtime.

### 3. Fleet Context & Negative Space (P0)
*   **Avoid Monolithic Dependencies:** The trend away from large, all-in-one frameworks (like early LangChain) is confirmed. Our fleet's architecture—specialized components (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`, `cudaclaw`)—is aligned with the industry's composable direction.
*   **Avoid Pure Cloud Assumptions:** The rise of edge/GPU-native runtimes indicates that architectures assuming always-on, high-bandwidth cloud LLM calls may face limitations. Our fleet's inclusion of `JetsonClaw1` (edge GPU) and `Forgemaster` (RTX 4050) is positioned in a growing channel.
*   **Observation on Training:** The concept of **self-improving agent rooms** (`plato-torch` presets) is not yet a mainstream pattern in observed open-source projects. Most public frameworks treat agents as static code, not as systems that generate their own training data ("tiles"). This could be a unique fleet advantage or a P0 integration challenge.
