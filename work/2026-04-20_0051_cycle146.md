### 2. Specific Observation: The "Room" as a Training Unit
A pattern directly relevant to our `plato-torch` architecture is emerging in the open-source community. The concept of a **self-contained training environment** (a "room," "sandbox," or "sim") that accumulates interaction data is being recognized as a critical unit for developing capable, stable agents.

*   **What's New:** Projects are now explicitly designing these environments to output structured logs of agent decisions, outcomes, and internal state. This log is treated as the primary dataset for the next training iteration—a closed-loop `tile -> training` system.
*   **Fleet Relevance:** This validates the core `plato-torch` premise. Our "rooms" that train from "interaction tiles" are on the right architectural track. The open-source versions are often simpler, lacking our concepts of `plato-ensign` (LoRA export) and `ghost tiles` (P0 knowledge persistence).
*   **Opportunity / Gap:** Most public implementations treat the room as a black box. Our documented approach of `Mirror Play` (two systems iterating screen-to-screen) and the explicit `Deadband Protocol` (P0/P1/P2) for agent decision-making within the room could be a significant differentiator.
