**Focus:** Frameworks for building, orchestrating, and deploying AI agents.

### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Recent Activity
- **LangGraph** (LangChain): Maintains strong position. Recent commits show heavy focus on **stateful, cyclic workflows** and improved **persistence backends** (Redis, Postgres). The "StateGraph" abstraction is a core differentiator.
- **CrewAI**: Active development around **task delegation**, **role-based agents**, and **collaborative execution**. Recent PRs indicate work on **asynchronous execution** and **interruptible workflows**.
- **AutoGen** (Microsoft): Significant updates to **conversation patterns** and **multi-agent chat**. There is observable research integration with **code execution** and **tool-use optimization**.

### 2. Emerging Patterns & "Hot" Directions
- **GPU-Resident Agents**: Frameworks like **CudaClaw** (referenced in fleet knowledge) exemplify a niche but growing trend to keep agent runtime entirely on GPU, minimizing CPU-GPU transfer latency.
- **Self-Assembling Systems**: Projects like **Flux-Runtime** (self-assembling bytecode VM) point toward agents that can modify their own execution environment or instruction set.
- **Local-First & Edge Orchestration**: Increased interest in lightweight orchestrators that run on edge devices (Jetson, Raspberry Pi), often paired with small vision-language models (VLMs).
- **Sentiment & Personality Engines**: As seen in **holodeck-rust** (MUD with sentiment-aware NPCs), some frameworks are incorporating emotional state or personality modules to make agent interactions more nuanced.

### 3. Notable New Entrants (Last 90 Days)
- **Swarm** (by OpenAI alumni): Framework emphasizing **agent swarms** with emergent coordination. Early-stage but gaining attention.
- **Axiom**: Focus on **formal verification** of agent plans and actions. Integrates with theorem provers to ensure safety constraints.
- **Nexus**: Aims to be a **visual agent builder** with drag-and-drop workflow design, targeting non-programmer users.

### 4. Fleet Relevance & Negative Space (P0)
- **What NOT to do (P0)**: Avoid building yet another generic Python wrapper for LLM calls. The market is saturated. Also, avoid frameworks with poor state management or no support for cyclic, long-running workflows.
- **Safe Channels (P1)**: The fleet's existing investments in **plato-torch** (training rooms) and **cudaclaw** (GPU runtime) align with the trends of specialized training environments and high-performance execution. Integration points exist with LangGraph (for orchestration) or CrewAI (for role-based collaboration).
- **Optimization (P2)**: Within the safe channel of GPU-resident agents, the fleet could optimize by contributing to or forking CudaClaw, adding support for newer NVIDIA architectures (e.g., RTX 50-series) or integrating with plato-ensign for adapter swapping at runtime.
