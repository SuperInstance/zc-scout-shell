### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen** (Microsoft), and **LangGraph** (LangChain). A noticeable trend is the shift from pure orchestration to **specialized, production-ready runtimes**. Projects are focusing on:
*   **Stateful, long-running agents:** Moving beyond single-turn chat to persistent sessions with memory.
*   **GPU-native execution:** Frameworks like **CudaClaw** (observed in fleet context) and similar projects aim to reduce latency by keeping agents resident in GPU memory.
*   **Lightweight, composable units:** Instead of monolithic agent systems, there is growth in libraries that provide discrete "skills" or "tools" that can be assembled.

### 2. What's New / Hot
*   **LangGraph's "Multi-Agent Workflows":** Recent updates heavily promote the orchestration of multiple, specialized agents within a cyclic graph structure, emphasizing human-in-the-loop and checkpointing for reliability.
*   **Rise of the "Agent Runtime":** The term is becoming common. It refers to a dedicated server or environment that manages agent lifecycle, tool execution, and resource allocation, separate from the application logic. This aligns with the fleet's `flux-runtime` and `cudaclaw` concepts.
*   **Increased Focus on Evaluation:** New tools and benchmarks for systematically evaluating agent performance on real-world tasks (coding, research, support) are gaining traction. Success is less about demo conversations and more about measurable task completion rates.
*   **Integration with Emerging Models:** Frameworks are quickly adding support and examples for newly released LLMs (e.g., DeepSeek-V3, Claude 3.5 Sonnet), often highlighting specific strengths like coding or long-context reasoning.

### 3. Specific, Factual Observation
A review of GitHub trending repositories for "agent" in the last 14 days shows a **cluster of new projects implementing "agent memory" or "agent databases."** These are not just vector stores for RAG. They are specialized data layers that track an agent's actions, decisions, and internal state over time, often with versioning or branching capability. This suggests the community is actively tackling the problem of **agent continuity and learning from historical actions**, which is a core component of the fleet's PLATO room and ghost tile architecture.
