### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph**. However, the trend is shifting from monolithic orchestration toward **specialized, composable modules**. Newer projects focus on specific niches: **memory management**, **tool execution sandboxing**, and **low-latency inference**.

### 2. Notable New Entrants & Trends
- **SWE-agent** (from Princeton) continues to gain traction for code-repair tasks, with recent benchmarks showing improved performance on SWE-bench.
- **Aider** is trending for its CLI-first, git-aware approach to pair programming with LLMs.
- **OpenDevin** remains active but development has slowed; the community is fragmenting into smaller, focused forks.
- **NVIDIA's NIM** microservices are influencing design patterns, pushing frameworks toward containerized, GPU-optimized inference endpoints.
- **Rust-based runtimes** are emerging for performance-critical agent components (e.g., **candle-agent**), aligning with the fleet's `cudaclaw` and `holodeck-rust` direction.

### 3. Integration & Interoperability
The "framework war" is cooling. The new focus is on **standardized APIs** (OpenAI-compatible, LiteLLM) and **protocols** (SSE, WebSocket for streaming). Projects increasingly act as **pluggable components** rather than end-to-end platforms. This matches the fleet's architectural principle of rooms and ensigns as exportable modules.

### 4. Research Frontier

- **Constraint-based reasoning** (relevant to `constraint-theory-core`) for improving agent planning reliability.
- **Self-correction loops** where agents critique and revise their own outputs, a pattern seen in the fleet's Mirror Play and Deadband Protocol (P0/P1/P2).
- **Multi-agent simulation** for generating training data (`fleet-simulator` alignment).

The fleet's `plato-ensign` (LoRA adapters from room experience) and `ghost tiles` (P0 knowledge preservation) represent a distinct, potentially advantageous trajectory.
