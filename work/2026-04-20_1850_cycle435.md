### 2. What's New/Hot (April 2026)

Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in our own fleet context) and similar community efforts (**LocalAgent**, **Inferix**) are gaining attention. The focus is on agent runtimes that reside on GPU memory to reduce latency.
*   **Constraint-Based Orchestration:** Inspired by geometric constraint-solving (see our `constraint-theory-core`), some experimental frameworks are applying these principles to agent task planning. This is a small but growing research thread, moving away from pure LLM-based planning to more deterministic, verifiable task graphs.
*   **"Self-Assembling" Systems:** The concept behind **flux-runtime** (self-assembling bytecode VM) is mirrored in community projects exploring agents that can reconfigure their own communication pathways and tool sets based on the task, without pre-defined crew structures.
*   **MUDs as Agent Environments:** The use of Multi-User Dungeons (**MUDs**) as lightweight, sentiment-aware simulation environments for agent training (like our `holodeck-rust`) is being discussed as an alternative to heavy 3D simulators for social reasoning training.
