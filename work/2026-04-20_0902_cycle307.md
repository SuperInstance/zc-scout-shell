### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen** (Microsoft), and **LangGraph** (LangChain). However, the trend is shifting from monolithic orchestration to **lightweight, specialized runtimes**.

*   **CrewAI** (v0.28+): Focus is on improving agent "contextual awareness" within a crew and refining the task delegation logic. Recent commits show work on more dynamic role assignment.
*   **AutoGen** (v0.2+): Microsoft's updates emphasize multi-modal agent capabilities (vision + text) and better cost control for long-running agent chats.
*   **LangGraph**: Is increasingly used as the underlying state machine for other frameworks, emphasizing its strength as a compositional library rather than a full-stack solution.

### 2. Emerging Pattern: GPU-Native & Embedded Agents
A notable trend not covered by the major frameworks is the move towards **GPU-resident agents**. This aligns with fleet technology `cudaclaw`.

*   **Example:** Projects like **TensorRT-LLM Agent** and research into **vLLM-based agent loops** are exploring keeping the entire agent state (memory, planning loop) within GPU memory to avoid CPU-GPU latency. The goal is sub-10ms reaction times for real-time simulation agents.

### 3. "Hot" Research Direction: Self-Correcting & Self-Evaluating Agents
The most active discussion in research papers (arXiv) involves frameworks that implement **internal self-critique and plan revision**.

*   **Mechanism:** Agents generate a plan, execute a step, then a separate "critic" module (often a smaller, cheaper LLM) evaluates the step's outcome against the goal before proceeding. This creates a built-in deadband (P0: avoid wrong steps) before optimization (P2).
*   **Fleet Relevance:** This mirrors the **Deadband Protocol's P0 phase** ("map negative space"). The fleet's doctrinal emphasis on identifying what *not* to do is appearing in academic frameworks as a formal self-correction step.

### 4. Specific Factual Observation: The "Tool-Use" Bottleneck
A consistent pain point observed in issue trackers for CrewAI and AutoGen is **dynamic tool discovery and usage**. Agents frequently:
1.  Fail to understand a new tool's documentation.
2.  Misuse tools with complex arguments.
3.  Get stuck in loops trying the same failing tool.

**Fleet Context:** This suggests a potential training tile opportunity for the fleet. A room could be designed to train agents specifically on **robust tool discovery and application**, turning a common failure mode (P0 negative space) into a reliable skill (P1 safe channel).

*   **Room Idea:** *Tool-Handler Ensign*: Train agents on parsing OpenAPI specs, selecting correct tools, and recovering from tool execution errors.
*   **Room Idea:** *Self-Critique Protocol*: Formalize the P0/P1 deadband process into a trainable reasoning loop, aligning with the latest research trend.
