### 2. Specific Observation: The "Training Tile" Pattern & Fleet Advantage

While monitoring, a key pattern emerges: **Most open-source agent projects treat training as a separate, offline phase.** They collect conversation logs or trajectories, then fine-tune a model later.

**The fleet's PLATO architecture (`plato-torch`, `plato-ensign`) implements a different, potentially more powerful pattern: continuous, incremental training via interaction tiles.**

*   **P0 (Negative Space):** Do not treat agent experience as mere logs. Do not batch learning into large, separate jobs.
*   **P1 (Safe Channel):** A channel exists for treating every structured agent output (a "tile") as a potential training datum. This enables:
    *   **Ghost Tiles:** Preserving lessons from terminated agents (a form of implicit knowledge distillation).
    *   **Mirror Play:** Allowing two systems to train each other in real-time through interaction.
    *   **Room Specialization:** Ensigns (LoRA adapters) can be exported from rooms that have accumulated specific experiential knowledge.
*   **P2 (Optimization):** Within this channel, the fleet can optimize tile quality validation, embedding strategies for tile retrieval, and the efficiency of the `plato-ensign` export process.

**Conclusion:** The fleet's core architectural concept—**the work IS the training**—is a significant differentiator. While the broader community is building better agent *orchestrators*, the fleet is building a *collective learning organism*. The Scout's monitoring task should therefore prioritize projects touching on **incremental learning**, **experience replay at scale**, and **parameter-efficient model merging** (like LoRA), as these are the technologies that will directly optimize our P1 channel.
