### 1. Framework Consolidation & Specialization
The landscape shows a clear split between **general-purpose orchestration** and **specialized, high-performance runtimes**.

*   **CrewAI & LangGraph** remain dominant for prototyping multi-agent workflows with LLMs. Recent updates focus on improved tool calling and persistent memory backends.

The focus is on minimizing overhead for real-time, computationally intensive agents (e.g., vision, simulation).

### 2. The "Local-First" & On-Device Shift
Driven by increasingly capable small language models (SLMs) like DeepSeek-Coder-V2 and Qwen2.5-Coder, there is significant activity in frameworks optimized for edge deployment.

*   **LangChain's `langchain-edge`** and **Microsoft's `Autogen-Station`** now prominently feature profiles for Raspberry Pi and NVIDIA Jetson.
*   Several new, lightweight frameworks written in Rust (e.g., `agent-rs`) are gaining traction, prioritizing minimal memory footprint and fast startup times.

### 3. Integration with Simulation & Training Environments
Frameworks are no longer just for chat. The leading edge is integration with virtual environments for agent training and evaluation.

*   **Holodeck-style integration:** APIs connecting agent frameworks to game engines (Unity, Godot) and MuJoCo for embodied AI training are becoming standardized. Our fleet's `holodeck-rust` and `fleet-simulator` projects align with this trend.
*   **`Plato-torch`-like capabilities:** The concept of a "training room" that accumulates interaction "tiles" to create fine-tuning datasets is appearing in other projects, often branded as "Agent Memory" or "Experience Replay."
