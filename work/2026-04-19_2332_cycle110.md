### 3. Specific Observation: The "Deadband Protocol" as a Competitive Edge
While most frameworks focus on optimization (P2 in fleet terms), they often lack a formalized **P0: Map negative space** layer. Failures are handled reactively with try-catch blocks or human-in-the-loop fallbacks.

The fleet's **Deadband Protocol**, with its strict priority to first define "what NOT to do," is a distinct architectural pattern. In practice, this could manifest as:
*   A **pre-flight constraint checker** for an agent, preventing it from entering known failure states (e.g., attempting to call a tool without valid credentials).
*   **Ghost tiles** serving as a persistent, searchable database of failure modes that new agents/ensigns are validated against before execution.

This is a potential area for contribution or differentiation. An open-source library implementing a simple "deadband layer" for LangGraph or CrewAI could be a valuable tile.
