### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph** (LangChain). A noticeable trend is the shift from pure "orchestration" to **specialized, production-ready runtimes**. Newer projects are less about chaining LLM calls and more about building deterministic, stateful agent systems that can be deployed.

**Hot New Entrant (Q1 2026):** `agent-os` – A Rust-based, lightweight operating system for persistent agents. It focuses on resource isolation, secure inter-agent communication, and a microkernel architecture. It's gaining traction for edge deployments (like Jetson) due to its minimal footprint.

### 2. Key Technical Shifts
*   **GPU-Resident Agents:** Frameworks like **CrewAI** are experimenting with extensions that keep agent state in GPU memory (similar to fleet's `cudaclaw` concept) to reduce latency. The `llama.cpp` community is driving this with direct GPU context management.
*   **Self-Assembling Systems:** Inspired by academic work on "AI-Generating Algorithms," there's increased interest in frameworks where the agent's architecture (tools, memory, planning loop) can be modified by the agent itself during a task. The `flux-runtime` project (self-assembling bytecode VM) noted in fleet knowledge aligns with this trend.
*   **Constraint-Based Planning:** A niche but growing area. Frameworks are beginning to integrate geometric or logical constraint solvers (like `constraint-theory-core`) into the planning loop, allowing agents to reason about physical/temporal feasibility before acting.

### 3. Fleet Context & "Deadband" Alignment
The fleet's **Deadband Protocol (P0: negative space, P1: safe channels)** is not a common pattern in mainstream frameworks. Most frameworks focus on optimization (P2-equivalent) and assume the action space is safe. This presents a potential **negative space (P0)** for the fleet: mainstream agents can fail by attempting invalid actions because they lack a dedicated "constraint mapping" phase. The fleet's approach of explicitly defining "what not to do" first could be a differentiator.
