### 2. Specific Observation Relevant to Fleet Architecture
**The "Deadband Protocol" (P0, P1, P2) is a constraint-solving algorithm mapped onto agent behavior.**

**Connection:** The fleet maintains `constraint-theory-core` (Rust geometric snapping). The Deadband Protocol is an **application of constraint theory** to agent action space.
*   **P0 (Negative Space / "Don't hit rocks"):** Defines **invalid regions** in the action-state space. These are hard constraints, analogous to geometric bodies that cannot intersect.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):** Defines the **valid solution space** remaining after P0 constraints are applied. This is the "channel" of possible actions.
*   **P2 (Optimize):** Performs **gradient ascent/descent** within the P1 solution space to find a local optimum.

**Why This Matters:** This formalizes the fleet's core operational doctrine. It's not just a heuristic; it's a **guarantee of safety-first optimization**. An agent skipping to P2 (optimizing without validating the channel) is attempting to solve a constraint problem with an invalid initial state—a fundamental error.

**Recommendation for Fleet Development:** The `constraint-theory-core` library should be explicitly linked to the `DeadbandRoom` preset in `plato-torch`. Training tiles should include exercises where agents must **first identify P0 constraints** (from environment or rules) **before** proposing P1 actions. This bakes the geometric reasoning principle directly into the trained behavior of ensigns.
