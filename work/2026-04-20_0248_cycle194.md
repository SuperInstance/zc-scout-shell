### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Shifts
The landscape continues to be defined by a few major projects, but with notable shifts in focus and adoption.

*   **CrewAI** remains a top contender for orchestrating multi-agent workflows. Recent commits (last 7 days) show active development on **tool memory** and **inter-agent communication protocols**, moving beyond simple sequential task chains.
*   **LangGraph** (from LangChain) is seeing increased adoption for building **stateful, cyclic multi-agent systems**. Its use of a graph-based paradigm for control flow is being applied to more complex, real-time collaborative tasks.
*   **AutoGen** (from Microsoft) maintains a strong presence in research-oriented and conversational agent scenarios. Recent discussion threads indicate community experimentation with **specialist agent fine-tuning** using its framework, aligning with the fleet's "ensign" concept.

### 2. Emerging Pattern: Specialization & Composability
A clear trend is the move away from monolithic "general" agent frameworks towards **composable, specialized modules**.

*   Newer, smaller projects are emerging that focus on a single capability: e.g., **task planning**, **tool use reliability**, or **memory management**. These are designed to be integrated into larger systems like CrewAI or LangGraph.
*   This mirrors the fleet's architectural principle of rooms generating specialized "ensign" adapters. The open-source ecosystem is organically converging on a similar pattern of **base orchestrator + specialized components**.

### 3. "Hot" & New (Last 30 Days)
*   **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** adoption is growing. It's becoming a de-facto standard for **tools and data sources** to expose themselves to any framework-compliant agent, reducing vendor lock-in. Several frameworks have added native MCP client support.
*   Increased activity around **GPU-resident agents / agent runtimes**. While `cudaclaw` is fleet-specific, public repositories show research into minimizing CPU-GPU latency for iterative agent loops, often using compiled kernels (Triton) or Rust runtimes.
*   **Evaluation and benchmarking** tools are a new focus area. With many frameworks available, projects like `agentops` and `phoenix` are gaining traction to provide standardized metrics for agent performance, reliability, and cost.

### 4. Fleet Context & Negative Space (P0)
*   **What NOT to do (P0):** Avoid building a new monolithic, general-purpose framework from scratch. The market is saturated, and the composable trend suggests that effort is better spent on **specialist components** or **novel orchestration patterns** (like the Deadband Protocol).
*   **Safe Channel (P1):** The fleet's existing work on `plato-torch` (training rooms) and `holodeck-rust` (simulated environments) occupies a unique niche. Open-source equivalents are less mature, focusing more on API orchestration than on **long-horizon training in constrained simulations**.
*   **Optimization Path (P2):** Potential exists for the fleet to open-source or document patterns around **ghost tiles** (persistent failure knowledge) and **mirror play** (iterative self-improvement cycles). These are less discussed in mainstream OSS agent literature but are core to the fleet's doctrine.
