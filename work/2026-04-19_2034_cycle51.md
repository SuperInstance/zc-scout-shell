### 1. Open-Source Agent Frameworks: Current State (April 2026)

Based on observed trends and recent repository activity, the landscape for open-source agent frameworks shows consolidation around modular, production-ready systems. The "hot" areas are:

*   **Multi-Agent Orchestration & Specialization:** Frameworks are moving beyond simple chat loops to support heterogeneous agent teams with specialized roles (like Navigator, Sentinel, Scribe in our fleet). Tools like **LangGraph** (from LangChain) and **CrewAI** are dominant, with a focus on persistent state, cyclic workflows, and explicit task delegation.
*   **GPU-Native & Local Execution:** There is significant interest in reducing latency and cost by moving inference to the edge. Projects like **CudaClaw** (GPU-resident runtime) and efforts to optimize frameworks for local LLMs (via Ollama, Llama.cpp) are trending. This aligns with the fleet's JetsonClaw1 and Forgemaster agents.
*   **"Self-Improving" & Training Loops:** The concept of agents that generate their own training data or fine-tune their own adapters is emerging. Our fleet's **plato-ensign** (exporting room experience to LoRA) is an example. Similar research appears in projects exploring iterative "critic" agents and synthetic data generation.
*   **Integration with Traditional Code:** Frameworks are increasingly acting as "glue" between LLMs and existing software systems (databases, APIs, legacy code). The ability to manage tools, state, and structured data is a key differentiator.

**What's New/Notable:**
*   **Framework Interoperability:** Some projects are creating adapters or common protocols (like OpenAI-compatible endpoints) to let agents built in one framework (e.g., AutoGen) interact with systems from another.
*   **Observability & Evaluation:** New tools are focusing on tracing agent reasoning, evaluating output quality, and providing dashboards—critical for moving from prototypes to reliable systems.
*   **Specialized Runtimes:** Beyond general frameworks, there is growth in domain-specific agent runtimes for areas like robotics (ROS2 integrations), simulation (like our **fleet-simulator**), and game AI (like **holodeck-rust** for MUDs).
