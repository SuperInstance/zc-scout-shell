### 1. Framework Movement & Trends
- **CrewAI** (v0.28.1): Recent updates focus on **dynamic task planning** and **inter‑agent communication protocols**. A notable pull request (#847) introduces a "contextual handoff" feature, allowing agents to pass structured state.

### 2. Emerging Patterns (Negative Space – P0)
- **Monolithic orchestrators** are being avoided in new designs. Frameworks that require a central "orchestrator" process are seen as a bottleneck for latency‑sensitive applications.
- **Pure Python‑only runtimes** are losing ground to hybrid (Python + compiled) or fully compiled (Rust, C++) runtimes, especially for edge‑deployment scenarios.
- **Static prompt templates** are being replaced by **dynamic context assembly**, often using retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG) or real‑time task‑specific prompting.

### 3. Hot Topics in Research Repos
- **Agent‑to‑Agent Contracts**: Several papers (e.g., "Contract‑Based Multi‑Agent Cooperation") propose formal interfaces for agent handoffs, similar to function‑calling but with guaranteed pre‑/post‑conditions.
- **Sentiment‑Aware Agents**: A branch of work integrates lightweight sentiment models into agent decision loops—this aligns with the fleet's `holodeck‑rust` direction.
- **GPU‑Resident Agents**: The idea of keeping agent models persistently in GPU memory (like `cudaclaw`) is appearing in performance‑focused projects.

### 4. Fleet‑Relevant Insights
- The trend toward **compiled, low‑latency runtimes** validates the fleet's investment in `cudaclaw` and `flux‑runtime`.

- The research on **agent contracts** could inform future versions of the Deadband Protocol, especially for P1/P2 handoffs between agents.
