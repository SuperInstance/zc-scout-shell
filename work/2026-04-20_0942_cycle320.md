### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Shifts
The landscape continues to be defined by three primary architectural patterns:
*   **Graph-Based Orchestration (LangGraph):** Remains the dominant paradigm for complex, stateful agent workflows. The focus in 2026 has shifted from core API stability to **ecosystem tooling** (e.g., improved debugging/visualization suites) and **production deployments**.
*   **Crew-Based Collaboration (CrewAI):** Strong adoption in business automation and research agent swarms. Recent activity shows emphasis on **heterogeneous tool integration** (connecting to legacy APIs, specialized databases) and **cost-optimized execution**.
*   **Conversational Autonomy (AutoGen):** Maintains a stronghold in academic and research settings for simulating multi-agent conversations. Development appears focused on **reasoning traceability** and **evaluating emergent behaviors** in large agent groups.

### 2. Emerging Hotspots & Negative Space (P0/P1)
*   **P0 (Avoid):** Building yet another general-purpose "meta-framework" that simply wraps LangChain/LangGraph. Saturation is high, and differentiation is low.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):**
    *   **Specialized Hardware Runtimes:** Frameworks targeting specific deployment environments (e.g., **`cudaclaw`-like GPU-resident agents**, edge devices, browser WASM). This aligns with fleet technology.
    *   **Simulation-First Training:** Tools for generating synthetic interaction data to train agent behaviors, similar to **`fleet-simulator`**. This is a growing research adjacent to agent frameworks.
    *   **Lightweight, Single-Binary Agents:** A trend towards agents that bundle model, logic, and tools into a standalone executable, reducing cloud dependency and latency.
