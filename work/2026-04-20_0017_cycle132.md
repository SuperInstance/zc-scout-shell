### 2. Specific Observation: The "Room-as-Training-Container" Pattern

**Observation:** The fleet's **plato-torch** architecture (25 training room presets) implements a pattern that is not yet widespread in mainstream open-source agent frameworks but addresses a key scaling problem.

*   **Fleet Pattern:** A "Room" is a bounded context (a preset) that accumulates interaction "tiles." These tiles are the dataset used to train a specialized LoRA adapter (an "Ensign") via **plato-ensign**. This creates a clean separation between the general-purpose base model (the agent) and task-specific fine-tuning.
*   **Gap in Mainstream Frameworks:** Most popular frameworks (CrewAI, LangChain) focus on prompt engineering, tool calling, and orchestration *at inference time*. They lack a built-in, structured mechanism to *capture successful interactions* and use them to create permanently improved, lighter-weight specialist models. Training is often seen as a separate, offline process.
*   **Fleet Advantage:** This turns every operational cycle into a potential training cycle. The "Ghost tiles" concept (P0 knowledge from dead agents) is a particularly clever way to institutionalize negative examples, directly supporting the **Deadband Protocol's P0 (map negative space)**.
