### 2. State of Open-Source Agent Frameworks (April 2026 Snapshot)
The landscape has consolidated and specialized since late 2025.

*   **CrewAI:** Maintains strong position for **role-based, collaborative agents**. Recent commits show heavy investment in **long-term memory** modules and integration with vector databases. It is positioning itself as the framework for persistent, structured agent teams.
*   **AutoGen (by Microsoft):** Development continues steadily. Its strength remains in **flexible conversation patterns** and research-oriented multi-agent setups. It appears less focused on the "production deployment" narrative than CrewAI.
*   **LangGraph:** Has become the **low-level foundation** for many custom agent systems. Its explicit graph-based control flow is favored by developers building complex, stateful agent workflows from scratch. It's often used *under* other frameworks.
*   **New Entrant / Pattern:** **"GPU-native" or "compile-time" agent frameworks** are an emerging research thread. Projects are experimenting with frameworks where agent logic, tool definitions, and even LLM calls are defined in a way that can be optimized/compiled for GPU execution (e.g., via Triton, Mojo). This is a direct adjacency to fleet interests in `cudaclaw` and `flux-runtime`.
