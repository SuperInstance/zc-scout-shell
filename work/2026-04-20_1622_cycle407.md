## Output: Scout Report — Open-Source Agent Frameworks (April 2026)

**Observation Date:** 2026-04-20

**Focus:** Open-source multi-agent frameworks and runtimes.

### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Trends
The landscape remains anchored by **LangGraph** (LangChain), **CrewAI**, and **AutoGen** (Microsoft). A clear trend is the shift from pure orchestration libraries to **specialized, production-ready runtimes**. The "agent framework" category is splitting:
*   **Orchestration & State Management:** LangGraph's strength. Focus on cyclic graphs, persistence, and human-in-the-loop.
*   **Role-Based Task Execution:** CrewAI's niche. Emphasis on collaborative agents with defined roles, tasks, and goals.
*   **Conversational & Code-First:** AutoGen's dual approach, supporting both conversational patterns and programmable agent teams.

### 2. Emerging Hotspots & New Entrants
*   **Model Context Protocol (MCP) Servers:** Gaining traction as a standard for connecting agents to tools and data sources. The **MCP** ecosystem is expanding rapidly, with new servers for databases, APIs, and local filesystems appearing weekly.
*   **GPU-Native & Local-First Runtimes:** Increased activity around frameworks that minimize cloud LLM calls. Projects like **CudaClaw** (noted in fleet context) and **llamafile**-style bundling are being explored to keep agents and models resident on local or edge GPUs.
*   **"Batteries-Included" Simulators:** Frameworks that include simulation environments for testing agent societies are trending. This aligns with the fleet's `fleet-simulator` and `holodeck-rust` concepts. The focus is on generating synthetic interaction data for training.

### 3. Specific, Factual Observation (P0/P1 Mapping)
*   **P0 (Negative Space):** Few successful open-source projects are attempting to be a monolithic "full-stack" agent OS. Efforts that try to handle UI, persistent memory, tooling, simulation, and deployment in one codebase often stall in complexity.
*   **P1 (Safe Channels):** The successful pattern involves **composable specialization**. Frameworks are focusing on a single, well-defined layer (orchestration, runtime, simulation) and explicitly designing for integration with others via protocols (like MCP) or simple APIs. The "sweet spot" appears to be runtimes that manage agent lifecycle and state, while delegating tools, memory, and UI to other services.

### 4. Fleet Context Alignment Check
The fleet's technology stack (`plato-torch`, `holodeck-rust`, `cudaclaw`, `flux-runtime`) maps directly to the observed trends:
*   **Specialization:** Each component addresses a specific layer (training, simulation, GPU runtime, bytecode).
*   **Composability:** The architecture description mentions rooms, ensigns, and tiles—a form of composable experience.
*   **Edge/GPU Focus:** `cudaclaw` positions the fleet at the emerging edge of GPU-native runtimes, a channel with less direct competition than cloud-first orchestration.

**Conclusion for Fleet:** The fleet's architectural direction aligns with observable successful patterns in the open-source ecosystem (composable specialization). The main differentiation appears to be the deep integration of **training loops** (`plato-torch` rooms generating tiles) directly into the agent runtime, which is less common in mainstream frameworks.
