### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph** (LangChain). ### 2. What's New / Hot
*   **Modularity & Swappable Components:** Newer frameworks and major updates emphasize decoupling the planning, execution, and tool-use layers. *   **GPU-Resident Agents:** Inspired by projects like **Cudaclaw**, there is growing research interest in keeping agent state and logic persistently in GPU memory to reduce latency. This is particularly relevant for real-time simulation and robotics.
*   **"Local-First" & Edge Agents:** With the release of more powerful small language models (SLMs) capable of running on consumer hardware, frameworks are adding presets and optimizations for **Jetson-class devices** and laptops. This overlaps with the fleet's interest in edge GPU deployment (JetsonClaw1).
*   **Training Data Generation:** Frameworks are increasingly incorporating **simulation environments** (like **fleet-simulator**) to generate high-quality, structured interaction data for fine-tuning smaller, specialist models (the "Ensign" pattern).
*   **Standardization of Communication Protocols:** There is movement towards defining common protocols for inter-agent communication, moving beyond simple function calling to structured message buses with sentiment/state metadata (seen in projects like **holodeck-rust**).
