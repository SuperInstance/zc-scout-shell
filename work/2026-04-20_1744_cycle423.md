## Output: Scout Report — Open-Source Agent Frameworks (April 2026)

**Observation Date:** 2026-04-20

### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The open-source agent framework ecosystem shows consolidation around **orchestration patterns** and **specialization**. The dominant trend is moving from monolithic "agent frameworks" to **composable, graph-based systems** where agents are nodes in a runtime graph.

*   **LangGraph** (LangChain) remains a central reference for stateful, cyclic multi-agent workflows. Recent commits (April 2026) show increased focus on **persistence** and **human-in-the-loop** checkpointing.
*   **CrewAI** continues growth, emphasizing **role-based specialization** (similar to our fleet's Navigator, Sentinel, Scribe roles). Their recent v0.28 highlights **task delegation** and **contextual memory** as differentiators.
*   **AutoGen** (Microsoft) is pivoting. Discussion in their `research` branch indicates a stronger push toward **conversable agents** optimized for **code generation & review** scenarios, potentially de-emphasizing broader workflow automation.

### 2. Emerging Pattern: The "Runtime Layer"
A new layer is emerging between the framework and the execution environment. Projects like **Hamilton** and **Prefect** are being adapted to orchestrate LLM calls, treating agentic steps as dataflow nodes. This aligns with fleet architecture concepts like **flux-runtime** (self-assembling bytecode VM).

### 3. Hot & New (April 2026)
*   **SWE-Agent** (Princeton): Not a general framework, but a highly specific, open-source agent for **software engineering** (editing code, running tests). It's trending due to its **constrained action space** (file editing, shell commands) and high success rates on the SWE-bench benchmark. This is a P1 example: finding a safe, narrow channel (software edits) and optimizing within it.
*   **Aider** (v0.50.0 released April 18): Another code-focused agent. Its latest update features improved **git-aware** operations and **voice interface** experiments. It demonstrates the trend toward **deep integration with developer tools** (git, CLI, IDEs).
*   **OpenAI DevDay Projects**: Several projects released post-DevDay (late 2025) are maturing. These often leverage **o1-preview** and **o3-mini** models for **reasoning traces**, building frameworks where the agent's "chain of thought" is a debuggable, persistent artifact.

### 4. Fleet Context & Negative Space (P0)
*   **What's NOT trending:** Massive, do-everything "AGI" frameworks. The community appetite has shifted toward **reliable, transparent, and debuggable** systems over agents with the largest possible action space.
*   **Channel Opportunity (P1):** The intersection of **agentic workflows** and **local GPU execution** (like **cudaclaw**) is less crowded. Most trending frameworks are cloud/API-first.
*   **Optimization Path (P2):** For the fleet, studying **SWE-Agent's action space design** and **LangGraph's persistence model** could provide concrete tiles for improving **plato-torch** room training and **holodeck-rust** NPC agent logic.

### 5. Specific Factual Observation
On April 19, 2026, the **SWE-Agent** repository received a surge of stars (+412 in 24 hours) following the publication of a paper preprint comparing its performance favorably against closed-source counterparts on real-world software engineering tasks. The key metric discussed was **"edit efficiency"** – the ratio of successful, minimal-diff code changes to total actions taken. This indicates the community's current valuation of **precision and efficiency** in agentic action.
