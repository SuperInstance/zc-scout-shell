### 2. What's "Hot" (High Discussion & Experimentation)
*   **Agent-to-Agent Evaluation**: There is growing discussion and prototype code around using a "judge" or "critic" agent to evaluate the output of a "worker" agent, creating self-improving loops. This is often implemented using LangGraph cycles or CrewAI's sequential process.
*   **Cost & Latency Optimization**: With increased usage, attention is turning to minimizing LLM calls. Techniques like "agent distillation" (training smaller models on successful agent trajectories) and better caching of intermediate results are frequent topics in repository issues and research papers.
*   **Integration with Existing Systems**: The "hot" feature is not new agent primitives, but robust connectors. Frameworks are competing on their built-in support for Slack, Discord, Gmail, Notion, and enterprise tools like Salesforce and ServiceNow.

### 3. Fleet Context Alignment & Negative Space (P0)
*   **P0 - What NOT to do:** Building yet another general-purpose Python orchestration framework from scratch appears to be a crowded and resource-intensive negative space. The market differentiators are no longer basic task delegation.

*   **Observation for the Fleet:** The trend towards **specialist agents** (smol agents, ensigns) and **persistent, trainable agent environments** (rooms, simulated fleets) aligns directly with our architectural pillars (plato-torch, fleet-simulator). Our work on capturing interactions as "tiles" for training is adjacent to the "agent distillation" research but is more focused on continuous, incremental learning within a bounded environment.
