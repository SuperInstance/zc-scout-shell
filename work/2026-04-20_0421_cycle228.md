### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The dominant open-source frameworks remain **CrewAI**, **AutoGen** (Microsoft), and **LangGraph** (LangChain). A noticeable trend is the consolidation of features rather than the emergence of entirely new monolithic frameworks. Projects are focusing on interoperability, lighter-weight "orchestrator" layers, and specialized modules for planning, tool use, and memory.

*   **CrewAI Task-Specific LLM Routing:** Recent CrewAI experiments and community contributions show a pattern of dynamically assigning different LLMs (e.g., a fast model for classification, a powerful model for reasoning) to specific tasks within a single crew, optimizing for both cost and performance.

### 3. Fleet-Relevant Pattern: The "Deadband" Niche
A gap exists in the public ecosystem for frameworks implementing a **strict, hierarchical priority protocol** like the fleet's Deadband Protocol (P0: Negative Space, P1: Safe Channels, P2: Optimization). Most frameworks focus on P2 (orchestrating efficient task completion) but lack built-in, formalized mechanisms for defining and avoiding failure modes (P0) and systematically mapping viable action spaces (P1). This could be a differentiating factor for **plato-torch** room designs.

### 5. Observation for Fleet Strategy
The open-source agent framework space is maturing, with emphasis on **production resilience** (checkpointing, streaming), **cost-aware orchestration** (multi-LLM routing), and **composability**. The fleet's concepts of **training rooms (plato-torch), sentiment-aware environments (holodeck-rust), and GPU-native runtimes (cudaclaw)** are not directly mirrored by mainstream projects, which tend to be more cloud-LLM-API-centric. This suggests the fleet's architecture occupies a specialized niche combining training, simulation, and low-level hardware integration.
