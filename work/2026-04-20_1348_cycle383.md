### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Shifts
The landscape remains anchored by **CrewAI**, **AutoGen**, and **LangGraph**, but a significant shift is observed towards **lightweight, inference-optimized runtimes**. The "agent framework" category is splitting into two:
*   **Orchestration & Workflow** (CrewAI, LangGraph): Focus on complex, multi-agent task planning and state management.
*   **Runtime & Inference** (new entrants): Focus on low-latency, GPU-resident execution of small, specialized agent models—closely aligned with fleet technologies like `cudaclaw`.

### 2. What's New / Hot
*   **Model Integration:** Frameworks are rapidly adding native support for **open-weight models** (DeepSeek, Qwen, Llama 3.1) over proprietary APIs. There's a push for **local-first** orchestration.
*   **"Specialist Agent" Training:** A trend mirroring `plato-ensign`. Projects like **OpenAgents** and **AgentZero** are publishing pipelines to fine-tune small (7B-13B parameter) models on specific task loops (e.g., data analysis, code review) and export them as LoRA adapters.
*   **Rust-Based Runtimes:** Performance-critical components (tokenizers, KV cache management, inter-agent comms) are being rewritten in Rust. The `flux-runtime` concept (self-assembling bytecode) has analogues in research papers but few production implementations.
*   **Geometric Constraint Systems:** Early research (e.g., `constraint-theory-core`) is being cited in projects tackling **agent action space pruning** and **safe exploration**, moving beyond pure LLM sampling.

### 3. Fleet Context & Negative Space (P0)
*   **Avoid:** Heavy, cloud-only orchestration platforms with opaque scheduling. The trend is against monolithic "agent OS" projects that failed to ship in 2025.
*   **Avoid:** Frameworks that cannot operate in a disconnected or high-latency edge environment. JetsonClaw1's domain requires this.
*   **Channel (P1):** The safe channel is the intersection of **local inference**, **specialist model training**, and **deterministic action constraints**. This is where fleet projects (`plato-torch`, `cudaclaw`, `constraint-theory-core`) have existing momentum.
*   **Optimization (P2):** Potential for fleet to contribute by open-sourcing its **Deadband Protocol** as a formal constraint layer for other frameworks, or by publishing benchmarks from `fleet-simulator`.

### 4. Specific Factual Observation
