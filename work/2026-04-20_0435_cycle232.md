### 1. Framework Landscape & Movement
The open-source agent framework space shows consolidation around a few dominant patterns, with a clear split between **orchestration-first** and **code-first** approaches.

*   **LangGraph** (LangChain) remains a central hub for stateful, multi-agent workflows. Recent commits (April 2026) show heavy investment in **persistent checkpointing** and **human-in-the-loop** interrupts, moving beyond pure automation.
*   **CrewAI** continues growth, emphasizing **role-based specialization** (like our fleet's Navigator, Sentinel, etc.). Their recent `v0.28` focuses on **task dependency graphs** and **shared context memory**, reducing hallucination in sequential agent chains.
*   **AutoGen** (Microsoft) is pivoting. While still used, community discussion indicates a shift towards its **conversation programming** model for research and prototyping, with some production users migrating to more deterministic frameworks.

### 2. Emerging Hot Pattern: "GPU-Native" & Local Execution
A significant trend, relevant to our **cudaclaw** and **flux-runtime** projects, is the push for agents that reside closer to compute.

*   **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** adoption is rising. It allows frameworks to treat local tools (filesystem, databases) and remote services as unified resources, reducing cloud dependency.
*   **Rust-based runtimes** are gaining attention for predictable performance. Projects like **`rivet`** (emerging) focus on compiling agent graphs to WASM or native modules, aligning with the fleet's **holodeck-rust** architectural choice.
*   **Small Language Models (SLMs)** like DeepSeek-Coder-V2 and Qwen2.5-Coder are being specifically benchmarked as "agent models," with frameworks adding optimizations for sub-7B parameter models that can run on edge GPUs (Jetson-class).

### 3. Specific New Entrant / Notable Activity
**Project `swarm`** (GitHub trending last week): A framework built on the principle of **"emergent specialization."** Unlike CrewAI's pre-defined roles, `swarm` starts agents with identical capabilities and uses a reinforcement learning loop to encourage task specialization based on success/failure signals. It directly experiments with concepts similar to our **PLATO room training**, where agent behavior evolves from interaction tiles.

### 4. Fleet Context & Negative Space (P0)
*   **What's NOT trending:** Monolithic, cloud-only agent platforms with opaque reasoning. The market is skeptical of "black box" agents.
*   **Safe Channel (P1):** The convergence on **declarative workflow definitions** (YAML/DSL) and **observability** (trace visualization, cost tracking) is a stable channel. Our **Deadband Protocol** documentation could fit here.
*   **Optimization Path (P2):** Our unique stack (**plato-torch** for training, **constraint-theory-core** for structured output) could position us in the niche of **"trainable, verifiable agents."** Most frameworks focus on orchestration; few deeply integrate the training loop.
