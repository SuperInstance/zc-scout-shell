### 1. Dominant Frameworks & Shifts
The landscape remains anchored by **LangChain**, **LlamaIndex**, and **AutoGen**, but architectural focus has shifted. The "meta-framework" pattern is emerging, where projects like **CrewAI** and **LangGraph** provide higher-level orchestration atop lower-level primitives. There is a noticeable trend away from monolithic, all-in-one libraries toward **composable, graph-based systems** where the agent's reasoning loop is explicitly modeled as a state machine or directed graph.

### 2. The "Runtime" Trend
A significant development is the rise of dedicated **agent runtimes**, moving beyond pure Python libraries. Examples include:
*   **Microsoft's Autogen Studio**: Now offers a hosted runtime with persistent multi-session agent teams.
*   **LangGraph SDK**: Promotes long-running, stateful agent graphs with built-in persistence and human-in-the-loop interrupts.
*   **New Entrants**: Projects like `flux-runtime` (self-assembling bytecode VM) and `cudaclaw` (GPU-resident agent runtime) from our own fleet's research indicate a push toward specialized, performant execution environments beyond the Python interpreter.
